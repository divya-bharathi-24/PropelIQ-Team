# User Story - US_018

## Story ID

- ID Format: US_018

## Story Title

- Walk-In Booking & Queue Management

## Description

- As a front-desk staff member, I want to register walk-in patients, add them to a real-time queue, and manage queue priority and wait times, so that walk-in patients are served efficiently without disrupting scheduled appointments.

## Acceptance Criteria

### AC-1: Walk-in patient quick registration

- **Given**: A patient walks in without a prior appointment
- **When**: The staff member selects "Walk-In Registration"
- **Then**: A streamlined form captures minimum required fields (name, phone, reason for visit, urgency level), creates a queue entry within 15 seconds, and assigns a queue number

### AC-2: Real-time queue display

- **Given**: There are patients in the walk-in queue
- **When**: The staff views the queue management screen
- **Then**: All queued patients are displayed in priority order with queue number, patient name, reason, urgency (Low/Medium/High/Urgent), estimated wait time, and time-in-queue, updating in real-time via WebSocket

### AC-3: Queue priority management

- **Given**: A staff member identifies a patient needing urgent attention
- **When**: They change a patient's urgency level from "Low" to "Urgent"
- **Then**: The patient is repositioned in the queue based on priority rules (Urgent > High > Medium > Low, FIFO within same priority), and estimated wait times recalculate for all queued patients

### AC-4: Patient called from queue and slot assigned

- **Given**: A provider becomes available
- **When**: Staff clicks "Call Next" or selects a specific patient
- **Then**: The patient status changes to "In Service", a slot is dynamically created in the provider's schedule, the patient is removed from the visible queue, and a check-in time is recorded

### AC-5: Estimated wait time calculation

- **Given**: Patients are in the queue
- **When**: The queue is viewed or updated
- **Then**: Wait time estimates are calculated based on average service time per provider (rolling 7-day average), current queue depth at each priority level, and number of available providers, displayed in minutes

## Edge Cases

- Walk-in patient leaves without being seen → staff marks as "Left Without Being Seen" (LWBS); queue position is freed; event logged for reporting
- All providers busy with scheduled patients → walk-in queue pauses advancement; estimated wait times increase; staff can view provider schedule to identify next available gap
- Queue grows beyond 15 patients → alert manager with recommendation to open additional provider capacity or redirect to partner clinic
