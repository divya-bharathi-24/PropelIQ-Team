# User Story - US_019

## Story ID

- ID Format: US_019

## Story Title

- No-Show Risk Scoring

## Description

- As a clinic manager, I want AI-powered no-show risk scores computed for each upcoming appointment based on patient history, appointment attributes, and behavioral patterns, so that proactive interventions can be targeted at high-risk appointments to reduce no-show rates.

## Acceptance Criteria

### AC-1: Risk score computed for every confirmed appointment

- **Given**: An appointment is confirmed and more than 24 hours in the future
- **When**: The daily risk scoring batch job runs (Hangfire, 6 AM daily)
- **Then**: A risk score (0-100) is computed using Gemini AI with structured output mode, factoring in patient's historical no-show rate, appointment lead time, day of week, time of day, and weather forecast

### AC-2: Risk categorization with thresholds

- **Given**: A risk score is computed for an appointment
- **When**: The score is persisted
- **Then**: It is categorized as Low (0-30), Medium (31-60), or High (61-100), and only Medium and High risk appointments are flagged for intervention in the provider dashboard

### AC-3: Risk factors explainability

- **Given**: A provider views a high-risk appointment
- **When**: They click "View Risk Details"
- **Then**: The top 3 contributing factors are displayed with relative weight (e.g., "Previous no-shows (2/5 past): 45%, Monday morning slot: 30%, Long lead time (14 days): 25%")

### AC-4: Risk score triggers reminder escalation

- **Given**: An appointment is scored as High risk (61-100)
- **When**: The score is persisted
- **Then**: The reminder schedule for that appointment is escalated: additional reminder at 48 hours before, follow-up call task created for staff, and appointment flagged with red indicator in provider view

### AC-5: Polly circuit breaker for Gemini API failures

- **Given**: The Gemini API is experiencing failures (3 consecutive errors)
- **When**: The circuit breaker opens
- **Then**: Risk scoring falls back to a rule-based model (historical no-show percentage only) for 60 seconds, then half-opens with a test request; scores computed during fallback are marked as "estimated"

## Edge Cases

- New patient with no history → assign medium risk (50) by default; after 3 appointments, historical model activates
- Patient's risk decreases after confirming (responded to reminder) → re-score within 1 hour of confirmation response; update category if changed
- Batch job runs during database maintenance window → retry after 30 minutes; alert operations if two consecutive failures
